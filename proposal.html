Project Proposal: Wildfire Reporter

The Problem

Wildfires are a growing threat to the safety of people and properties alike, all across the United States, and especially in western states like Washington, Oregon, and California. While Central and Eastern Washington have always struggled with fires, they've began to spread farther West as we see from fires like the Bolt Creek fire in 2022. With climate change we can expect to see these fires getting more and more common in areas less and less prepared to deal with it. One of the first steps to keeping up with the increasing wildfire threat is being prepared, and that's something we aim to help with.

Past Solutions

Many web apps exist for fire tracking, take for example FireWeatherAvalanche.org. this page has an interactive map that gives lots of information on fire and other severe weather all across the US. Its very handy for checking fire status anywhere and provides excellent fire statistics, but it doesn't include fire advice or alert you of fire proximity, you have to sort through the information it presents to know if the fire is near you and how concerned you should be (it doesn't) give evacuation levels for areas, for example). Our goal is to make a more personal and more targeted web app aimed at keeping Washington residents aware of and prepared for fire threats!

Our Solution

We plan to make a web application that helps keep people aware of fires near them, evacuation levels, and gives advice on what action to take based on proximity to a fire. The app will also allow users to report fire incidents near now, or suspected fire incidets. This app will be accessible to everyone in Washington, and potentially even more of the US!

Functionality

  • User will be able to register for an account, to track their chosen address for fire alerts
  • User will be able to post suspected fire sitings on the site, or certain users (maybe admin?) will be able to post official fire updates on the site. Fire alert messages will be sent to people with addresses logged near the location
  • Users will have access to a variety of fire / evacuation advice, with certain advice being reccomended at closer proximity to fire. We plan to source fire safety information from the US Fire Administration

Conclusion

By making users more aware of fire danger through alerts (not using javascript alerts, don't worry), enabling quick official reporting of potential fires, and presenting up-to-date fire safety information we hope to make fire season safer for everyone in Washington State
